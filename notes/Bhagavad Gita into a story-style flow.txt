## 🌄 Story Format Version (Combined Context)

The battlefield of Kurukshetra is set. Conches are blown. Armies stand ready. On one side are the **Pandavas**, and on the other, the **Kauravas**.

Prince **Arjuna**, the greatest warrior of his time, asks his charioteer — Lord **Krishna** — to place the chariot between the two armies so he can observe who he must fight.

But what he sees shocks him.

He sees:

* His grandfather Bhishma
* His teacher Drona
* His cousins
* His friends
* His relatives

Suddenly, the war is no longer about justice. It becomes personal.

His hands begin to tremble.
His mouth becomes dry.
His bow (Gandiva) slips from his hands.

Arjuna’s mind fills with doubt:

> “How can I kill my own family for a kingdom?”
> “What joy will we get after destroying our own people?”
> “Even if they are wrong, is war the right solution?”

He argues that:

* Family destruction will destroy traditions.
* Society will collapse.
* Sin will fall upon them.

Overwhelmed by grief and confusion, Arjuna sits down in his chariot and refuses to fight.

This emotional breakdown becomes the starting point of the Gita.

👉 Chapter 1 is not weakness.
It shows a real human crisis — moral confusion before a major life decision.
